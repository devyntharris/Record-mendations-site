Modern

The Crux
  1. Gap Tooth Smile
  2. Link
  3. Delete Ya

The Crux is a 2025 album by Djo, the music project of Joe Keery (also known for his role in Stranger Things). The album blends vintage sounds from the '60s and '70s with modern indie pop, creating a rich and emotional atmosphere. Each song represents a different character or moment of change, inspired by Keery’s personal experiences, like a breakup and moving to a new city. Tracks like “Basic Being Basic” and “Delete Ya” explore themes of identity, love, and growth. Overall, The Crux shows Keery’s evolution as a musician and storyteller, and it’s been well-received for its creativity and heart.

Short n' Sweet
  1. Juno
  2. Taste
  3. Coincidence

Short n’ Sweet is a 2024 album by Sabrina Carpenter, blending playful pop with elements of disco, rock, and R&B. Known for its sharp wit and emotional honesty, the album explores love, heartbreak, and self-discovery through catchy, clever lyrics and vibrant production. Tracks like “Espresso” and “Please Please Please” highlight Carpenter’s humor and confidence, while deeper cuts like “Lie to Girls” reveal a more introspective side. With its bold sound and personal storytelling, Short n’ Sweet marks a major step forward in Carpenter’s evolution as a pop artist.

Starcatcher
  1. Farewell for now
  2. Meeting the master
  3. Runaway Blues

Starcatcher is a 2023 album by Greta Van Fleet, an American rock band known for their retro sound. The album blends classic rock influences—especially from bands like Led Zeppelin—with mystical and fantasy-inspired themes. It features powerful vocals, heavy guitar riffs, and big, dramatic energy. Songs like “Meeting the Master” and “The Falling Sky” explore ideas of mythology, spirituality, and searching for truth. Starcatcher continues the band’s signature style while showing their growth in storytelling and sound.

Sour
  1. Traitor
  2. Favorite Crime
  3. Happier

Sour is the 2021 debut album by Olivia Rodrigo, blending pop, alt-rock, and stripped-down ballads to deliver a raw and emotionally charged portrait of heartbreak, insecurity, and growing up. Across its eleven tracks, Rodrigo explores the highs and lows of teenage emotions with striking honesty, weaving personal storytelling into universally relatable themes. Songs like “drivers license,” “deja vu,” and “good 4 u” highlight her ability to shift between vulnerability and rage, making Sour a defining debut that resonated deeply with listeners and critics alike.

Inside Deluxe
  1. Goodbye
  2. All Eyes On Me
  3. 1985

Inside (Deluxe)" expands Bo Burnham’s original 2021 concept album with unreleased tracks, alternate versions, and behind-the-scenes audio that deepen the emotional and thematic landscape of his pandemic-era project. Retaining the raw introspection, biting satire, and genre-blending style of the original Inside, this extended edition offers a fuller picture of Burnham’s creative process and evolving mental state. From darkly comic explorations of internet culture to haunting reflections on isolation and identity, Inside (Deluxe) is a layered and unfiltered chronicle of art-making in confinement—both more intimate and more expansive than before.

Hollywood's Bleeding
  1. Goodbyes
  2. Myself
  3. Circles

Hollywood’s Bleeding is a 2019 genre-blending album by Post Malone that combines hip-hop, rock, and pop to explore themes of isolation, fame, and emotional decay beneath the surface of celebrity life. The album features a wide range of collaborators, including Travis Scott, Halsey, and Ozzy Osbourne, creating a diverse sonic landscape that shifts between melancholy reflection and upbeat energy. With standout tracks like “Circles,” “Sunflower,” and “Take What You Want,” Hollywood’s Bleeding captures the tension between success and emptiness, offering one of Post Malone’s most cohesive and introspective projects to date.

Oldies

The Essential Elvis Presley
  1. Moody blue
  2. Return to Sender
  3. If I Can Dream

The Essential Elvis Presley is a 2007 compilation album that captures the evolution of Elvis Presley’s iconic career. Spanning hits from the 1950s to the 1970s, it features genre-defining tracks like “Hound Dog,” “Jailhouse Rock,” and “Suspicious Minds.” Blending rock and roll, gospel, blues, and pop, the album reflects both Presley’s musical range and the cultural shifts of his time. Each song offers a glimpse into different phases of his life and artistry, making it a powerful tribute to the King of Rock and Roll.

The Final Countdown
  1. Carrie
  2. The Final Countdown
  3. Rock The Night

The Final Countdown is a 1986 album by the Swedish rock band Europe. It’s best known for its iconic title track, which became a global hit with its catchy synth riff and epic feel. The album mixes glam metal and hard rock, with songs about love, adventure, and chasing dreams. Other popular tracks include “Carrie” and “Rock the Night.” With its big sound and powerful vocals, The Final Countdown helped Europe gain international fame and became one of the defining rock albums of the '80s.

ABBA Gold: Greatest Hits
  1. Voulez-Vous
  2. Chiquitita
  3. Gimme Gimme

ABBA Gold: Greatest Hits is a 1992 compilation album that celebrates the Swedish pop group ABBA’s most iconic tracks. Featuring timeless songs like “Dancing Queen,” “Mamma Mia,” and “Waterloo,” the album captures the group’s signature blend of catchy melodies, rich harmonies, and disco-pop energy. Spanning their most successful years, it reflects themes of love, heartbreak, and joy with a theatrical flair. ABBA Gold remains one of the best-selling albums of all time and stands as a definitive collection of the band’s enduring influence on pop music.

Sgt.Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band
  1. Being for the Benefit of Mr. Kite!
  2. A Day in the Life
  3. Fixing A Hole

Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band is the groundbreaking 1967 album by The Beatles, often hailed as one of the most influential records in music history. Framed as a fictional band’s performance, it blends psychedelic rock, orchestral pop, and experimental sounds to create a vivid, surreal listening experience. Tracks like “Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds” and “A Day in the Life” explore themes of imagination, identity, and societal change. Fueled by studio innovation and cultural curiosity, Sgt. Pepper marked a turning point in The Beatles’ artistry and reshaped the possibilities of the album as an art form.

Ritchie Valens
  1. Oh Donna
  2. We Belong Together
  3. Hi-Tone

Ritchie Valens is the 1959 debut and only studio album released during the life of rock and roll pioneer Ritchie Valens. Featuring hits like “La Bamba,” “Come On, Let’s Go,” and “Donna,” the album blends early rock, rhythm and blues, and Latin influences, capturing Valens’ vibrant energy and cultural crossover appeal. At just 17 years old, Valens brought a fresh, youthful voice to the late-1950s music scene, breaking barriers for Mexican-American artists. Though his career was tragically cut short, Ritchie Valens remains a powerful testament to his talent, charm, and lasting impact on rock history.

Film

Arcane: Season 2
  1. Paint the town blue
  2. Fantastic
  3. Come Play

The Arcane: Season 2 score builds on the atmospheric richness of the series’ first season, deepening its emotional intensity and narrative complexity. With a dynamic blend of orchestral arrangements, industrial tones, and ambient textures, the music underscores the chaos unfolding between Piltover and Zaun. The score balances sweeping cinematic moments with intimate character themes, giving voice to inner turmoil, betrayal, and fragile hope. Whether heightening a tense confrontation or illuminating a quiet emotional beat, the soundtrack expands the immersive world of Arcane, solidifying its place as a genre-defying audiovisual experience.

Stranger Things
  1. kids
  2. stranger things
  3. The upside down

Kyle Dixon and Michael Stein’s score for Stranger Things: Season 1 is a synth-driven masterpiece that defines the show's retro-futuristic identity. Inspired by classic '80s sci-fi and horror, the soundtrack uses analog synths, pulsing rhythms, and minimalist motifs to evoke both suspense and nostalgia. Tracks like “Kids,” “Castle Byers,” and “She’ll Kill You” perfectly capture the emotional spectrum of the series—from wonder and innocence to creeping dread. The score plays a crucial role in shaping the show's atmosphere, making it feel both timeless and rooted in a specific cultural moment. It’s a haunting yet heartfelt backdrop to a story of friendship, fear, and the supernatural.

Fantastic Mr.Fox
  1. Mr.Fox in the fields
  2. Bean's Secret Cider Cellar
  3. Jimmy Squirrel and Co.

Alexandre Desplat’s score for Fantastic Mr. Fox is a witty, whimsical, and rhythmically inventive soundtrack that brings Wes Anderson’s animated world to life. Featuring a playful blend of strings, woodwinds, and percussion, the music dances with a mischief that mirrors the cleverness of its titular character. Tracks move with a refined eccentricity, echoing the stop-motion animation’s detailed precision and the story’s charm. Desplat captures both the pastoral beauty of the countryside and the underlying tension of wild animals trying to outsmart their human foes. It’s a quirky, sophisticated score that enhances the film’s humor and heart without ever overwhelming it.

Hocus Pocus
  1. Sarah's Theme
  2. Main Title
  3. I put a spell on you

John Debney’s score for Hocus Pocus is a delightful fusion of spooky mischief, magical energy, and whimsical charm. From its opening notes, the music conjures a Halloween atmosphere full of enchantment and adventure. Swirling strings, eerie choral cues, and jaunty themes highlight the playful villainy of the Sanderson Sisters and the eerie backdrop of 17th- and 20th-century Salem. Debney strikes a delicate balance between family-friendly fun and dark fantasy, crafting a score that is as memorable as the film itself. It enhances every moment of comedy, suspense, and wonder, making it a Halloween staple for generations.

Edward Scissor Hands
  1. Ice Dance
  2. Death!
  3. Storytime

Danny Elfman’s score for Edward Scissorhands is a lush, emotionally charged composition that beautifully captures the bittersweet tone of Tim Burton’s modern fairy tale. Featuring ethereal choirs, twinkling celestas, and haunting strings, Elfman’s music paints a portrait of Edward’s gentle soul and his alienation from the world around him. Tracks like “Ice Dance” and “The Grand Finale” are filled with longing, innocence, and aching beauty. The score moves seamlessly between gothic grandeur and tender simplicity, acting as the emotional heartbeat of the film. It’s widely considered one of Elfman’s most iconic works and remains a landmark in fantasy film scoring.

Labyrinth
  1. Magic Dance
  2. As the world falls down
  3. into the labyrinth

The Labyrinth soundtrack is a unique blend of theatrical pop and fantasy orchestration, featuring original songs by David Bowie and an instrumental score by Trevor Jones. Bowie’s contributions—including “Magic Dance,” “Within You,” and “As the World Falls Down”—infuse the film with his signature glam-rock sensibility and emotional depth. Trevor Jones’ orchestral themes complement Bowie’s music with sweeping, mysterious soundscapes that bring the enchanted labyrinth to life. Together, the soundtrack merges whimsy, darkness, and pop mystique, reflecting the film’s surreal tone and iconic visual style. It’s a cult classic album that remains essential listening for fans of fantasy, Bowie, and ‘80s cinema./p>

Musicals

Back to the future: the musical
  1. Put your mind to it
  2. my myopia
  3. it's only a matter of time

Back to the Future: The Musical is a dynamic stage adaptation of the iconic 1985 sci-fi comedy, with music by Alan Silvestri and lyrics by Glen Ballard. The cast recording blends brand-new songs with familiar classics like “The Power of Love,” capturing the spirit of adventure and heart of the original film. Through high-energy rock numbers, heartfelt ballads, and clever lyrical nods, the album brings the time-traveling tale of Marty McFly and Doc Brown to vibrant theatrical life. It’s a nostalgic yet fresh musical experience that pays loving tribute to a pop culture classic.

Elvis
  1. If I Can Dream (Maneskin)
  2. The King and I
  3. Vegas

The Elvis soundtrack, produced by Baz Luhrmann for his 2022 biopic, is a genre-blending tribute to the King of Rock and Roll. This expansive album pairs reimagined Elvis classics with bold new interpretations from contemporary artists like Doja Cat, Stevie Nicks, Eminem, and Måneskin. It reflects the film’s high-octane visual style, weaving together gospel, blues, soul, rock, and hip-hop to tell the story of Elvis Presley’s meteoric rise and cultural impact. It’s not just a soundtrack—it’s a recontextualization of his music for a new era, honoring the legend while pushing creative boundaries.

Mamma Mia
  1. Our Last Summer
  2. SOS
  3. Does Your Mother Know

Mamma Mia! The Movie Soundtrack brings the infectious joy of ABBA’s music to life through the performances of its star-studded cast, including Meryl Streep, Amanda Seyfried, and Colin Firth. Set against the backdrop of a sunlit Greek island, the album reinterprets beloved hits like “Super Trouper,” “SOS,” and “The Winner Takes It All” with theatrical flair and emotional resonance. Whether playful or poignant, each song advances the story of love, family, and self-discovery. With its irresistible mix of pop nostalgia and heartfelt performances, the album celebrates ABBA’s enduring legacy in a new cinematic context.

Hamilton
  1. Dear Theodosia
  2. What'd I miss
  3. You'll Be Back

Hamilton is a groundbreaking musical written by Lin-Manuel Miranda that fuses hip-hop, R&B, and traditional Broadway styles to retell the life of American founding father Alexander Hamilton. Performed by a diverse cast, the soundtrack presents history with contemporary urgency, turning political debates into lyrical battles and personal struggles into powerful anthems. Songs like “Alexander Hamilton,” “Non-Stop,” and “Burn” blend personal drama with revolutionary ideals, offering an emotionally charged, rhythmically complex portrait of ambition, sacrifice, and legacy. It’s an album that redefined musical theatre and brought a new generation into the world of Broadway.

Heathers: The Musical
  1. Meant To Be yours
  2. Freeze Your Brain
  3. Candy Store

Heathers: The Musical transforms the cult 1989 teen dark comedy into a fiercely clever and emotionally charged rock musical. Set in a high school dominated by the cruel trio of Heathers, the story follows Veronica Sawyer as she navigates popularity, morality, and murder. With standout tracks like “Candy Store,” “Beautiful,” and “Meant to Be Yours,” the album mixes biting satire with surprising vulnerability. It captures the chaos and confusion of adolescence, blending edgy humor with real emotional weight. Bold, campy, and unafraid to go dark, it’s a musical that dares to ask what it costs to fit in.

Shrek The Musical
  1. Build a Wall
  2. When Words Fall
  3. Travel Song

Shrek the Musical takes the beloved animated movie and transforms it into a heartfelt and hilarious stage experience. With music by Jeanine Tesori and lyrics by David Lindsay-Abaire, the album features original songs like “Who I’d Be,” “I Know It’s Today,” and “Freak Flag” that dive deeper into the inner lives of Shrek, Fiona, Donkey, and other fairy tale misfits. The soundtrack combines big Broadway energy with a quirky, tongue-in-cheek charm that celebrates self-acceptance and love in unexpected forms. Whimsical and surprisingly moving, it turns a familiar story into a musical full of laughs and heart.
